Festivals in Konkan

"Konkan shines brightest during its festivals."

Shimga

“Flames of faith — Shimga ignites hearts with tradition and triumph.”Shimga, the Konkan version of Holi, begins with the sacred ritual of Holika Dahan — a bonfire symbolizing the victory of good over evil. The entire village gathers around the flames, singing folk songs and performing traditional dances in a high-energy, spiritual celebration.

Ganpati

Ganesh Chaturthi festivities in full swing.A line of individuals carries brightly painted idols of Lord Ganesha on their heads and shoulders. Their clothes are dusted with what appears to be pink or red colored powder, often associated with festive celebrations in India. The setting seems to be a rural or semi-urban area with abundant trees and foliage.

Palki

“Grace in devotion — the Konkan Palkhi comes alive with rhythm and reverence.”This vibrant scene captures a traditional Palkhi procession, an integral part of Konkan’s folk festivals. Carried with devotion, the palkhi represents a deity and is accompanied by rhythmic Dhol-Tasha, vibrant costumes, and spirited dance — a true showcase of local culture and unity.
